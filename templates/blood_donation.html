Health & Wellness Guide

Before You Donate

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water before donating blood to keep your body hydrated.

Eat Iron Rich Food

Consume foods like spinach, beans and red meat to maintain iron levels.

Get Good Sleep

Ensure you have at least 7–8 hours of sleep before donating blood.

After You Donate

Avoid Heavy Work

Do not perform heavy lifting or strenuous activity for the next 24 hours.

Eat Snacks

Have sugary snacks or juice if you feel dizzy after donating.

Keep Bandage On

Keep the bandage on your arm for a few hours to prevent bleeding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does donating blood hurt? +

You may feel a small pinch when the needle is inserted, but most donors report little to no pain.

Is the equipment safe? +

Yes. All equipment used for blood donation is sterile, used once and safely disposed.

How long does the blood donation process take? +

The donation itself takes about 8–10 minutes. The entire process including rest takes about 30–40 minutes.

What does the Emergency tag mean? +

An emergency request means the patient urgently needs blood and nearby donors will be notified immediately.
